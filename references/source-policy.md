# Snacks Source Policy

This policy keeps the daily Snacks run broad enough to avoid missing major stories without turning aggregators into final sources.

## Goal

Find the important recent stories, verify them against better sources, and publish only compact briefings with concrete facts.

## Daily Discovery Workflow

1. Run the broad web search first across AI, robotics, chips, compute, software, tech business, diplomacy, conflicts, sanctions, and strategic technology.
2. Build a candidate pool of 15 to 25 stories before writing anything.
3. Run a coverage-gap check against trusted aggregation surfaces.
4. Add missing candidates only when they are recent, concrete, and meaningfully different from the existing pool.
5. Resolve every selected story to primary sources or direct reporting before it appears in `Sources`.

## Aggregation Surfaces

Use these as discovery and sanity-check surfaces, not as default citations:

- Tech and AI: Techmeme, Hacker News, The Batch, Latent Space, Ben's Bites, Import AI, Semafor Technology, The Information front page.
- General news: Google News, Reuters topic pages, AP topic pages, Bloomberg front page, Financial Times front page.
- Geopolitics: Memeorandum, Ground News, ISW, ACLED, Crisis Group, CFR, Atlantic Council, official UN/NATO/EU pages.
- Primary tech watchlist: OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, Meta AI, Microsoft, Nvidia, TSMC, ASML, AMD, Apple, Amazon, xAI, Mistral, Hugging Face, major model or product changelogs.

If an aggregation surface points to a story, follow the trail. The final link should usually be the original report, official announcement, filing, paper, court document, transcript, dataset, or agency story.

## Source Hierarchy

Prefer sources in this order:

1. Primary documents: official announcements, filings, legislation, court documents, company blogs, model cards, papers, government statements, datasets.
2. Direct reporting: the outlet or journalist that broke or materially reported the story.
3. Wires and specialist reporting: Reuters, AP, Bloomberg, Financial Times, The Information, TechCrunch, The Verge, Ars Technica, IEEE Spectrum, Defense News, Janes, Al Jazeera, BBC, NPR, Politico, Euractiv.
4. Aggregators and reposts: use only as pointers unless they add original analysis or unique context.

Avoid citing low-quality mirrors, SEO farms, unattributed summaries, or articles that only paraphrase another piece without adding reporting.

## Importance Filter

Prefer a candidate when it has at least two of these signals:

- A major actor is involved.
- The story changes capabilities, policy, market structure, conflict dynamics, or supply chains.
- It includes hard numbers, dates, documents, product names, model names, benchmarks, budgets, shipments, legal provisions, or casualty/impact figures.
- It is recurring across at least two trusted surfaces.
- It updates a story already followed by Snacks in the last few days.

Do not force a fixed number of stories per category. A thin day should stay thin.

## Coverage-Gap Check

Before writing:

- Compare the candidate pool with at least two tech/AI aggregation surfaces and two general or geopolitics surfaces.
- Look for repeated high-placement stories missing from the pool.
- For each missing story, either add it or reject it because it is stale, vague, duplicated, outside scope, or lacks source quality.
- Treat aggregator prominence as a signal to investigate, not proof that the story belongs in the briefing.

## Link Rules

- Use at least one primary or direct-reporting source per story whenever available.
- Use two sources when a story has both an official claim and independent reporting.
- Link to the aggregator only when it is itself the source of original reporting or analysis.
- Preserve exact publication dates, names, numbers, and institutional wording.
- If source quality is weak, skip the story rather than padding the briefing.

## Brevity Rules

- Target 90 to 130 words per story.
- Keep the hard limit at 150 words.
- Prefer two strong bullets over three average bullets.
- Keep sarcasm in the setup or kicker, not in factual claims.
